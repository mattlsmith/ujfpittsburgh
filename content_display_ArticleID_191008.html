"The Partnership Report"- An On The Scene Bulletin From Karmiel & Misgav

August 3, 2006


To our dear friends and partners,

Today is Thursday, the 23rd day of the war.

Last night passed quietly and this morning as well only a relatively "sparse" number of rockets fell on the communities of the north. However, as we moved into the afternoon hours and now as I write my daily report, the sounds of air raid sirens followed by explosions fill the air. It seems that we have begun to receive a heavy barrage of Katyusha rockets falling across the north.

In fact, from the report I have just received, a massive barrage of missiles has struck the region. Some 17 rounds fell in Karmiel alone, all of them in open fields; however, some came quite close to a number of residential neighborhoods. One rocket fell within 10 yards of a family's home, but luckily failed to explode.

Adi Eldar inspects damage caused by a Katyusha rocket
Three rounds also fell in a number of Misgav communities (Tsurit, Camun and Mitzpe Aviv). Here too, "lady luck" was on our side and the rockets fell in surrounding open fields.

It seems that the quiet we all hope for remains elusive, we must however be patient and continue to operate within the confines of the current "state of emergency". This includes staying in shelters or secure zones, maintaining those shelters, repairing property damage, responding to residents' needs and organizing safe, sufficient and enjoyable activities for our children.


Safe and enjoyable children's activities in a shelter in the Misgav community of Koranit
A perfect example of this can be found in the Misgav Bedouin village of Hosinia. Following a second Katyusha rocket that fell on the village just next to the (thankfully) empty kindergarten (no injuries), a welcomed initiative was conceived and led by Gaphnit Shalev from Ma'ale Zvia with assistance from MATI "Lev Hagalil".

Before the war, a group of 24 women from Hosinia and an additional number of women from Ma'ale Zvia began a joint business development course aimed at providing them with the tools necessary to develop and succeed at building their own independent businesses. The outbreak of hostilities forced the women to put their studies "on hold", but at a joint gathering of support and consolation held by the "sisters in sorrow", they all promised to renew their studies as soon as possible and begin building their independent enterprises, in accordance with the program's original concept.

The entrepreneurs of Hosinia get a relaxing massage
Mosmiya Suad, an expert in medicinal herbs and spices, and one of the program's participants, has already initiated a preliminary effort at developing a business by hosting visiting groups (before the war) and received an enthusiastic response. The course, an initiative sponsored by MATI "Lev Hagalil" and the Misgav Regional Council is funded in part by the Pittsburgh UJF IEA Fund.

Another welcome initiative, one that I've reported on in the past, is an effort being made by artists from the Galilee to exhibit and sell their works at the weekend arts & crafts festivals held in Tel Aviv.

Maya Tsaban, press spokeswoman for the Misgav Regional Council reported that 10 artists from Misgav and the area participated in the weekend festivals and received tremendous positive feedback and support from a large numbers of visitors. On Friday, the artists were guests of the Nahalat Binyamin Boardwalk Arts Festival. On Saturday, they exhibited in the fashionable Port of Tel Aviv area.

All of the artists gave a positive report concerning the various events and noted the great amount of attention and encouragement that they received from the festivals' visitors. "Everyone was so enthusiastic", reported Adva Sinai, an artist from Mitzpe Aviv, "Finally someone brought them a different type of artwork, something that they were not use to seeing. Throughout the Friday event at Nahalat Binyamin, the organizers called people on loudspeakers to come and see our works, to come and meet artists from the Galilee. Many of the visitors from Tel Aviv had a lot of questions about the situation, how we were organized in terms of our kids and our homes, etc."

A Misgav artist exhibiting at a Tel Aviv Art's Festival
The artists also noted that many of the festival's visitors were themselves residents of the north who were proud to see a "northern contingent" among the exhibiting artists.

Another festival, especially for Galilee artists will be held on Friday August 4, 2006 in the Tel Aviv Port area.

The Karmiel Youth Village is an alternative child welfare framework for children unable to remain in permanent care of their biological parents. The village is composed of 10 residential family units (a couple + their own biological children) that are responsible for groups of up to 10 children ages 6-18 who have been removed from their previous homes following a child welfare evaluation and court order.

All of the children study in local Karmiel schools and after school return to their foster families in the Youth Village.

YAD Mission members contributing backpacks to the kids of the Karmiel Youth Village
Many of you have had the pleasure to visit this exciting and heartwarming village during your visits here to the region and most recently even contributing backpacks to the children for the coming school year.

With the outbreak of the war and rocket attacks on Karmiel, the children were sent to safer quarters.

Some were returned temporarily to their biological families, while others were sent to other "Youth Villages" in more serene and safer areas.

Over the coming week the educational staff will meet in advance of the new school year scheduled to open on September 1.

Razi Shoshani, the village's director reported that both the village's educational and administrative staffs are working intensely to adapt the village's five existing bomb shelters to the needs of the hour and be prepared for the beginning of the school year, including the much-anticipated return of the children.

The village also serves as "home" to a group of 17 "lone soldiers" (those serving in the IDF and have no family in Israel). The soldiers (when they are able to get out, which is not often because of their service and the situation) share a block of four apartments that were recently renovated thanks to a generous contribution made by members of the Pittsburgh Jewish Community and channeled through the UJF.   

That's all for today, let us pray that quiet will be restored and peace and security will return to the region and the country.

The next report will be issued on Monday, August 7.

Until then, Shabbat Shalom,
Yael Raich
Partnership 2000 Karmiel-Misgav-Pittsburgh/ the Jewish Agency for Israel
