UJF TRUSTEES APPROVE $11.5 MILLION GOAL FOR 2003 CAMPAIGN  UJF TRUSTEES APPROVE $11.5 MILLION GOAL FOR 2003 CAMPAIGN



UJF Trustees voted to approve an $11.5 million goal for the 2003 Annual Community Campaign. This comes as the 2003 Annual Campaign crosses the $4 million mark and heads toward Fundfest, the Federation's largest fund raising event of the year.

"There are still several months left in the Annual Campaign, but if our community works hard and gives to its full potential we will reach the $11.5 goal set forth by the UJF Trustees," said UJF Annual Community Campaign Chair Hannah Kamin.

"There is no question that this is a challenging year for fundraising," said UJF Development Chair, Richard Kann. "The turmoil in Israel continues and our economy is struggling, but we cannot let that impact our Annual Campaign.

"Our Annual Campaign meets the on-going needs of Jews who are struggling to survive in Pittsburgh, in Israel and around the world. We still need to feed and house the elderly, educate our children, absorb thousands of immigrants into Israeli society and care for and protect those who are unable to provide for themselves."

Funds from the Annual Community Campaign are being used in Israel to help support social services that have suffered severe economic cut backs due to increased security needs. The Annual Campaign helps meets the core social service needs of Israel's most vulnerable populations -- new immigrants who arrive by the hundreds every month, the 15% of Israelis who are unemployed and the thousands of Israeli children who now live below the poverty line.

"As we enter the most public phase of our campaign - - Fundfest -- we have the potential to impact the pace of the Campaign, while boosting our person-for-person increase," continued Kamin. "I want to encourage everyone in the community to participate in this week-long phoning effort, whether that means coming to Federation to make phone calls or answering your phone and making your commitment when a UJF volunteer calls you."

This year phonathons will be held at the UJF Building in Oakland on Sunday, February 2 from 9:30 a.m.-12:30 p.m. and 6:00 p.m.- 9:00 p.m. They will continue at the UJF on Tuesday, February 4 from 6:00 p.m.-9:00 p.m.; and Wednesday, February 5 from 6:00 p.m.- 9:00 p.m. A South Hills Jewish Community Center session will take place on Thursday, February 6th from 6:00 p.m.- 9:00 p.m.

For more information or to volunteer for Fundfest 2003, go to www.ujf.net or contact Samantha Kreindler at 412-992-5222 or skreindler@ujf.net.
