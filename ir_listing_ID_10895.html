Jewish Family & Children's Service

Website: http://www.jfcspgh.org
Contact: Maxine Horn

Jewish Family & Children's Service’s Older Adult Services department provides a number of services that help older adults, their families and caregivers get the support they need at various stages of the aging continuum, including Care Management (geriatric care managers are licensed social workers who make certain that their elderly clients receive the services they need to be safe, comfortable and maintain as much independence as their physical and mental health permits. Care managers work with older adults, their families, guardians, and others involved in their care to address their changing needs, help access relevant resources and maintain an ongoing relationship that provides comfort and security), the Caregiver Connection (matches JF&CS certified caregivers with older adults in need of personal care, housekeeping and light cleaning, companion services and respite care in their own home, assisted living or nursing home facility), Counseling & Support Groups (for older adults who might be depressed, anxious or trying to cope with the loss of independence or a loved one, JF&CS psychotherapists provide short- or long-term counseling one-on-one or in groups. For those unable to come to our office, home-based counseling services are available. Support groups are offered for bereavement, caregivers, and those affected by multiple sclerosis) and Holocaust Survivor Services (provides assistance for Holocaust survivors through care management and other services designed to help the elderly survivors live their remaining years safely, with dignity and as independently as possible. Underwritten by a grant from the Conference on Jewish Material Claims Against German, Inc. allowing JF&CS to provide service free of charge to participants).
