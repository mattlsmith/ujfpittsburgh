SHALOM SANKOFA TROLLEY TOUR

Sunday, October 2, 2005
2:00 p.m. at the Hill House - the focal point of the Hill District today

Space may still be available. Please call 412.992.5217 for more information. 

 

 

The SHALOM SANKOFA Trolley Tour is the first in an on-going series of SHALOM SANKOFA programs. 

 

The event will kickoff with Barbara Burstin, P.h.D, Lecturer on The United States and the Holocaust at The University of Pittsburgh and director “A Jewish Legacy: Pittsburgh”.  Dr. Burstin will introduce a 20 minute clip from the film which focuses on the Hill District and then answer questions following the screening.

 

Participants will then board trolleys for a tour of the Hill District including old synagogues and other Jewish landmarks.  The tour will be led by Mr. Nick Lane, a Jewish-Pittsburgh historian, with the assistance of Dr. Laurence Glasco, Associate Professor of Race & Ethnicity and Urban History at the University of Pittsburgh, who will lend his expertise on the African American history of the era. 

  

Both Shalom Pittsburgh and the Urban League Young Professionals of Pittsburgh are organizations of young professionals who feel a duty to shape the landscape of this region’s future.  By collaborating, we are taking the needed steps to revitalize discussions between our respective groups. 

 

Collaborative projects in this region will break barriers and provide opportunities for different minority groups to learn about each other.  Shalom Pittsburgh and ULYP-Pittsburgh desire to bring our young adult populations together beginning with the common theme of the Hill District.  SHALOM SANKOFA projects hope to revive the Black-Jewish Dialogue with young adults at the forefront.

 

For more information, contact Emily Farkas at efarkas@UJFpittsburgh.org or 412-992-5217.
