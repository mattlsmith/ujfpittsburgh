Mitzvah Day 2004



More than 300 volunteers participated in the annual UJF Mitzvah Day on December 24, serving at 21 human service agencies throughout the Pittsburgh area.

Mitzvah Day was organized by Shalom Pittsburgh, the United Jewish Federation Young Adult Division and the Jewish Community Center. It was sponsored by Citizens Bank, Executive Defensive Tactics, Highmark Blue Cross Blue Shield, The Giant Eagle Foundation, Arthurs Lestrange & Company, Littles Shoes in Squirrel Hill, Farkas Associates and Fine Design Kitchens and Baths.

The Jewish Community Center in Squirrel Hill opened its doors to Mitzvah Day volunteers all day, so that before or after their projects participants could meet, mingle and enjoy the day. The program began with a free breakfast at the JCC sponsored by Citizen's Bank. Among the volunteer sites shown below are: the Center for Creative Play, where volunteers painted, cleaned and prepared a new room for a grand opening; Debra House/Bridge to Independence, where volunteers cleaned and reorganized the interior of the facility, which offers transitional housing for homeless mothers; and the Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank, where volunteers helped sort and pack food for distribution to the community.
