"The Partnership Report"- An On The Scene Bulletin From Karmiel & Misgav

July 22, 2006


To our dear friends and partners,

Friday morning passed quietly. At last, we had the chance to get some fresh air, do some shopping and prepare for the Sabbath.

The Destruction Caused by a Katyusha Rocket Attack in Karmiel
We had hoped for a quiet and peaceful weekend; however, it was not to be. The serenity of the Sabbath was violently disturbed by two deadly rounds of Katyusha rockets. The first round struck at around 11AM Saturday morning and the second fell in the early evening hours, shortly before the end of the Sabbath.

Over 20 rockets struck the region, particularly in Karmiel where two homes and their occupants received direct hits. Ten residents were injured, one seriously. The others suffered lesser injuries and emotional stress. All were evacuated to the hospital in Nahariya in spite of the fact that it too is under attack.

Large numbers of reservists were called up over the Sabbath and in the coming days will take a more active role in the fighting to protect and defend the State of Israel.

As the war goes on more Karmiel and Misgav residents who until now had remained in the area, left their homes before the beginning of the Sabbath in the hope that they would be able to find more peaceful and calmer quarters. There is a limit to how much one can sit in a "secure room" or bomb shelter, anxiously waiting for the next round of rockets to strike. 
 
The View after a Direct Hit on a Karmiel Home
It is difficult to describe in writing the emptiness and desolation of Karmiel's streets and the "frightening quiet" of the communities of Misgav. Only a few cars can be seen on the roads, almost no one leaves their homes- many in fact are empty.

In an interview to Israel Television Adi Eldar, the mayor of Karmiel said, "in my worst nightmares, I never dreamed that my city of Karmiel would be placed in such a difficult situation. Nonetheless, I am proud of our residents who have shown their steadfastness and emotional strength and in spite of these difficult days, placed their faith and trust in the path and actions that the government has taken". 

Within the framework of the "personal diary" segment of our daily report, today I have selected a report from the members of the Misgav community of Kibbutz Eshbal. As members of this unique community, the residents are forced to cope with the situation on three varying levels. Everyone in the community must of course face the situation on a personal level.  Many are required to cope on an educational level, as educators in the community's Youth Village where they must show strength and support for the students living on the kibbutz. Lastly, there are those who are serving as youth movement counselors and being looked at as role models and for support by the hundreds of young members of the movement living in the war stricken Galilee.

The following are the words and experiences of the young people of Eshbal who have been forced to face 10 days of violent rocket attacks, fear and tremendous stress.

To All Of Our Friends Overseas,
 
Hello,
In these difficult times, we wanted to share with you our experiences as citizens of the north under Hezbollah attack.
 
Last Wednesday, when we began our day, no one could imagine that within a few hours we would be facing a completely different reality in which a third of the country's population is under attack, routine of life broken and mounting tension over a course of events whose length and outcome are unknown.
 
Youth Movement Activities in a Misgav Shelter
Only a week ago we were in the midst of summer activities: the boarding school students put on an end of the year performance and went to Kibbutz Ravid to pick lychee for a week. In the youth movement, the junior counselors' seminar had ended and summer day camps began for thousands of kids in Jewish and Arab cities and villages. At Eshbal, we began renovating our offices in anticipation of the next school year and members went on an educational trip to Poland in order to be certified as tour guides on journeys to Poland.
 
When the horrifying abduction and the military reaction began, it was still hard to conceive that it would last more than a day. Nevertheless, both the kibbutz and the youth movement "HaNoar HaOved V'HaLomed" began to prepare for a national emergency:

• The boarding school students were taken on a trip to the Jerusalem area. This weekend they will begin volunteering with the children of recent Ethiopian Olim (the Falashmora) who were evacuated from absorption centers in the north.
• Children who were at the movement's summer camp at Kfar Hahoresh were taken that night to an alternate site close to the Tel-Aviv area. The camp was also extended to keep the kids in safer surroundings for a few more days.
• We collaborated with the Jewish Agency to provide hundreds of children from the north free summer camps in the central region of Israel. The response was incredible and the parents were very grateful for the opportunity to send their children for a few days in the south after a whole week in bomb shelters.
• Our regular work with the youth in various communities continues in bomb shelters wherever possible. In some areas, travel is dangerous and the guides stay in the local bomb shelters themselves.
• Just as important, we are concerned with our own safety: We've set up a 24-hour "situation room" and are in contact with local security officials. We prepared secure rooms, as there are no bomb shelters at Eshbal. The council engineer advised us as to which rooms are safest and how to protect them. Most of the time we are in concrete structures as the caravans or offices provide little protection.
Still we look after the kibbutz and ourselves and maintain our spirits during these difficult days.
 
We would like to thank all those who sent emails of concern and support. It gives us strength knowing we are not alone in this.

We mourn the deaths of soldiers and civilians and keep the families of the kidnapped soldiers in our thoughts. We are very concerned for the young soldiers, some of them family members; others were our counselors, who are risking their lives to protect ours.

We hope this will all end soon and bring a new situation in the area: one of peace and security for everyone, on both sides of the border.

Sending you our love,
All members and students of Kibbutz Eshbal

That's all for today, let us pray that quiet will be restored and peace and security will return to the region and the country.

Sincerely,
Yael Raich
