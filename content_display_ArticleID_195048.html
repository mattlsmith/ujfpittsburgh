THREE MAJOR SYNAGOGUE MOVEMENTS
JOIN UJC IN ISRAEL EMERGENCY CAMPAIGN


Major Show of Solidarity

 

September 18, 2006

New York – Sept. 18, 2006 – In a show of solidarity as North American Jews mark the High Holy Days, the three major synagogue movements have announced support for the Israel Emergency Campaign of United Jewish Communities (UJC) and the Jewish Federations of North America.

Endorsements by the Union for Reform Judaism, United Synagogue for Conservative Judaism and the Orthodox Union clearly reflect the need for North American Jewry to come together in support of Israel following the 34 days of rocket attacks on northern Israel this past summer, UJC officials said.

“Support by our friends in the main synagogue movements reflects how North American Jews have coalesced around the Israel Emergency Campaign, and underscores our unwavering unity and solidarity with the people of Israel and the Jewish state during this critical time, and always,” said Robert Goldberg, UJC chair. “We are honored to stand shoulder to shoulder with the synagogue movements as we work together to respond to overwhelming needs in the Jewish homeland due to unprovoked attacks on Israel.”

The federation system and the synagogue movements, together, represent the largest Jewish constituent framework on the continent.  They are breaking new ground in their determination to broaden the overall base of support for Israel by creating a united front through the Israel Emergency Campaign.

The Israel Emergency Campaign, established shortly after Israel’s defensive war began in July, has so far raised more than $300 million for short- and long-term humanitarian needs and economic support for all Israelis – Jewish, Arab and Druze - victimized by the attacks.

“At this time, when Israel needs the support of the entire North American Jewish community, we commend UJC for its work in aiding the victims of the war and the rebuilding of Israel,” said Rabbi Eric Yoffie, president of the Union for Reform Judaism.

“The Conservative movement is proud to join United Jewish Communities in raising humanitarian funds to aid victims of terror, displaced families, and others in Israel who need our help,” said Rabbi Jerome Epstein, executive vice president of the United Synagogue of Conservative Judaism.

Rabbi Tzvi Hersh Weinreb, executive vice president of the Orthodox Union, said: “At this time of continuing and substantial need by our fellow Jews in Israel, the Orthodox Union is pleased to consolidate our efforts with those of United Jewish Communities.  Jewish unity is one of our people's greatest strengths in times of crisis.  Our national leadership and the Orthodox rabbinate will urge all those gathering in our congregations to support the Israel Emergency Campaign so that our Israeli brothers and sisters will receive the aid and comfort they require in the wake of the havoc brought upon them by Hezbollah's rockets.”

“Our partnership reflects the unity of the North American Jewish community and is a sign of our continual ability to stand as one and meet the challenge when Israel is in crisis,” said Howard M. Rieger, UJC president and chief executive officer. “As a result of this collaboration, we are also creating a powerful model for future cooperation and community building.”

Visit www.ujc.org for more information about the Israel Emergency Campaign or how to make a donation.

United Jewish Communities (UJC) represents 155 Jewish federations and 400 independent communities across North America. Through the UJA Federation Campaign, UJC provides life-saving and life-enhancing humanitarian assistance to those in need, and translates Jewish values into social action on behalf of millions of Jews in hundreds of communities in North America, in towns and villages throughout Israel, in the former Soviet Union, and 60 countries around the world.
