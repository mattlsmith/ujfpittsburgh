Involvement Opportunity Descriptions



Agency for Jewish Learning (AJL) - AJL is the central resource for Jewish education in Pittsburgh. It provides support to all of our Jewish learning providers, helping to train teachers at synagogue and Jewish day schools and create educational materials. AJL can coordinate two or more agencies working together to plan programs like community events celebrating Jewish holidays or Israel.  The agency offers classes of all kinds, and operates the Melton Institute, a two-year program that covers many important topics related to being Jewish. The School for Advanced Jewish Studies (SAJS), which is part of AJL, provides Jewish learning for teens.

Agency Operations Committee (AOC) - Lay leaders work closely with UJF beneficiary agencies to improve relations and financial practices. Macro initiatives include marketing, technology, joint purchasing, financial reporting, health care and Board governance. AOC seeks financial/ business savvy individuals. Community presence and perspective are helpful. Young leaders welcome.

Aging and Human Needs Commission - Set up to review priorities and determine allocations, this Commission examines the full scope of the community's needs and current services as it relates to aging and human needs. Overlapping services and unmet needs are identified and addressed by the commission in collaboration with the agencies. Main beneficiary agencies are: Jewish Agency on Aging, Jewish Residential Services, Jewish Family & Children's Services, and Riverview Towers.

Community and Public Affairs Council (CPAC) - CPAC meets at the intersection of Jewish values and public policy. The council seeks to build a Jewish community that is strong and influential in the arena of public affairs and seeks to build coalitions with people of all faiths in the joint pursuit of social justice. CPAC members discuss issues of concern to the Jewish community and help develop a social-action strategy for the United Jewish Federation. And as the public affairs arm of the UJF, the council communicates with elected officials at the local, state and national levels.

Day Schools (Community Day, Hillel, Yeshiva) - Jewish Day Schools offer programs in Hebrew language, Judaic studies and general studies.  Our community has three Day Schools. Community Day, for students from all Jewish backgrounds, has 320 students in K-8. Hillel Academy and Yeshiva Schools are affiliated with the Orthodox movement; Hillel has 200 students in pre-school through grade 12, and Yeshiva has 350 students in pre-school through grade 12.

Hillel Jewish University Center - Hillel JUC is the central address for Jewish life for college students on Pittsburgh area campuses. Hillel serves more than 3,000 Jewish students with social, educational, cultural, and religious programs.  The agency welcomes students from all backgrounds.

Holocaust Center - The Holocaust Center of Greater Pittsburgh is Western Pennsylvania's only educational resource center on the Holocaust. Opened in 1980, the center's mission is to educate teachers and students; to commemorate the lives of victims, including anyone traumatized by the events of 1933-1945; and to document those events through the collection of artifacts, videos and oral histories of survivors. The programs and exhibitions heighten public awareness, honor survivors, and keep the memory alive. The Holocaust center is looking for legacy individuals: people who may be 3rd or 4th generation from Holocaust victims/ survivors.

i-Connect - The i-connect project is a unique and multi-dimensional effort designed to intensify the significance of Israel in connecting Pittsburgh Jews to Judaism, community, the Jewish State and the Jewish people.  Includes Israeli-American women's dialogue group.

Israel and World Jewry Commission (IWJ) - Set up to review priorities and determine allocations, IWJ has the responsibility for planning and discussing all of the work done through the UJF concerning the Israel and World Jewry agenda.

Jewish Association on Aging (JAA) - JAA provides residential and outreach services for Pittsburgh’s Jewish elderly. The JAA provides a home and medical care for those who are too frail or ill to live on their own. JAA also provides many support services, such as Kosher meals on wheels, to enable others to continue to live in their own homes.

Jewish Community Center (JCC) - The JCC offers all kinds of programs for all age groups and segments of the Jewish community. In addition to year-round educational, cultural, social and fitness programs, the Center operates summer camp programs. Among the JCC’s most important programs are preschool, sports, art and other activities for school age children and families; teen trips to Israel; fitness programs; activities for children and adults with Special Needs; and much more.

Jewish Community Life Commission - Set up to review priorities and determine allocations, JCLC focuses on Jewish life in Pittsburgh. The main beneficiary agencies that JCLC reviews are: JCC, Hillel JUC, and Shalom Pittsburgh. The primary focuses of JCLC are teens and young adults with the objective to identify and involve this demographic.

Jewish Family & Children's Services (JF&CS) - JF&CS helps people with problems by providing counseling and other services.  The agency reaches out to families in crisis, like parents who are unemployed and cannot afford food or other necessities.  Counseling serves clients ranging from families who cannot get along to people who are dealing with illness or a death in the family.  JF&CS assists the elderly by helping them shop and do other chores, manage their money and get to doctor’s appointments.

Jewish Learning Commission (JLC) - Set up to review priorities and determine allocations, JLC is a commission very dedicated to the importance of Jewish learning. Their initiatives are to create a higher profile for Jewish learning, recruit and retain talented educators, and to ensure quality educational programming that meets children's needs. JLC beneficiary agencies include: Yeshiva, Mesivta, Hillel Academy, & Community Day.
Specifically JLC needs people who are passionate about Jewish learning.

Jewish Residential Services (JRS) - JRS provides housing and support services for people who need supervision and help in their everyday lives due to mental and physical disabilities.  JRS runs apartment buildings, with staff available to help residents build their living skills. Clients who need less support live on their own, with the help of other JRS services.  The Howard Levin Clubhouse gives people a comfortable lounge environment where they can socialize, but offers opportunities to learn work skills and social skills.  Programs such as holiday celebrations provide a Jewish connection for clients.

Partnership 2000 (P2K) - Participate in a variety of projects with economic development, educational and social components alongside people in our partnership region, Karmiel/ Misgav.

Pittsburgh's Jewish-Israeli Film Festival (PJIFF) - A three-week-long festival held annually that relies heavily on volunteer support from picking the films to staffing the viewings.

Riverview Towers - Riverview Towers provides apartments for the lower income elderly. Classes, outings and activities help residents stay busy, keeping them healthier.

Shalom Pittsburgh - Shalom Pittsburgh is the outreach and engagement arm of the UJF's Young Adult Division (YAD).  It plans dozens of programs and events yearly for singles, couples, and families with young children.  Shalom Pittsburgh also helps welcome newcomers to the community.

UJF Campaign - UJF could not do all that it does without community dollars and support. The campaign is very important to the UJF and is constantly looking for new people to assist in soliciting others for financial commitment.  Also, learn about Operation Promise, Cordozo Society (for Attorneys), and Maimonides Society (for Physicians).

UJF Women - UJF Women strives to raise the status of women and women's philanthropy in the Jewish community of Pittsburgh through social programming and education.  Our volunteers can plan and participate in programs on domestic violence, infertility, breast cancer awareness and so much more. Volunteers can also do hands-on tzedakah projects,  learn the techniques of successful solicitations and fundraising, collaborate with other Jewish organizations in the city on women's programming and work to build Kesher, people to people connections.

Young Adult Division (YAD) - The leadership of YAD focuses on two primary areas: Fundraising and Leadership Development (through the Wechsler Leadership Development Institute and Missions).
