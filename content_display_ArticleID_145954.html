Endowment Created for Yeshiva in Memory of Leonard Rudolph



The Yeshiva Schools of Pittsburgh has been named as the recipient of a significant endowment created by the Rudolph Family in memory of Leonard H. Rudolph.

 

The Leonard H. and Doris G. Rudolph Family Yeshiva Endowment Fund is the single largest endowment created as part of the United Jewish Federation Foundation's Agency Endowment Program. The Endowment fund will provide for the future vibrancy of the Yeshiva Schools' Wightman Street campus. 

 

The UJF Agency Endowment Program allows for the creation of endowments, through the UJF Foundation, that are directed specifically to the Federation's partner agencies.  The Rudolph Endowment is the 48th established through the program.

 

"There are three crowns," said Rabbi Yisroel Rosenfeld, dean of Yeshiva Schools,"the crown of Torah, the crown of priesthood, and the crown of kingship; but the crown of a good name surpasses them all.  Here in Pittsburgh, Leonard Rudolph’s name and the

Rudolph family name are names to be truly admired,” he said.

 

"Our Wightman Street facility will be named the Leonard H. Rudolph Building," Rabbi Rosenfeld said. "For over 40 years, Leonard Rudolph and the Rudolph family have been at the forefront of Jewish education.  Whether it was establishing a home for the Holocaust Center or chairing our annual scholarship dinners, the Rudolph family was right there, not just participating, but leading,” he added. "Yeshiva Schools is dedicated to producing Jewish leaders as well, so it is most appropriate that we name our building after the patriarch of the Rudolph family."

 

Jeff Finkelstein,UJF Chief Executive Officer, added, "The creation of the endowment and the naming of the Yeshiva Wightman Street building is a very fitting memorial to Leonard Rudolph. He valued Jewish education and set an extremely high standard for community leadership." 

 

From 1980 to 1982, Rudolph served as President of the United Jewish Federation, the top volunteer leadership position in the Jewish community of Pittsburgh.  He also served on more than a dozen boards and committees throughout the community.  He purchased the building that originally housed the Holocaust Center of the United Jewish Federation  and, with his wife Doris, established the Doris and Leonard H. Rudolph Jewish Communal Professional Award, an award that annually recognizes the exceptional personal and professional commitment of a Jewish communal professional.

 

"Perhaps most impressive," Rabbi Rosenfeld said, "is the way that Leonard's family has followed in his footsteps.  Jim Rudolph, also a former Chair of the United Jewish Federation has served as Chair of the Jewish Association on Aging and is current Chair of the Agency for Jewish Learning.  His brother, Bill, is president of Yeshiva Schools and is currently a Vice Chair of the United Jewish Federation.

 

To celebrate the naming, Yeshiva Schools will honor the memory of Leonard Rudolph at its 61st Annual Dinner, Monday, Feb. 28, 6 p.m. at the Pittsburgh Hilton Hotel.  Information and reservations can be obtained by calling Yeshiva Schools at 412-422-7300.

 

For more information about the UJF Agency Endowment Program, please contact Dan Brandeis or go to http://www.ujfpittsburgh.org/section_display.html?ID=254.
