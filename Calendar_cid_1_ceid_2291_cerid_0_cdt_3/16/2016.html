The Jewish Community Foundation & The Jewish Healthcare Foundation present

MEDICAL ETHICS IN JEWISH LAW
with Foundation Scholar
RABBI DR. DANNY SCHIFF

In this 3-part series, Rabbi Schiff will explore Jewish insights relating to some of the more challenging ethical issues that emerge within contemporary healthcare.  Rabbi Schiff will present compelling Jewish sources from ancient times through the twenty first century that will illuminate Jewish thinking on these difficult questions.  In each session, Rabbi Schiff will discuss a different issue, presenting a wonderful opportunity to engage in Jewish learning with a one-of-a-kind scholar.

  • Wednesday, February 17: END OF LIFE IN JEWISH LAW
  • Wednesday, March 16: ABORTION IN JEWISH LAW
  • Wednesday, May 25: TRIAGE - JEWISH LAW ON WHO SHALL LIVE WHEN NOT ALL CAN

Physicians:
This activity has been planned and implemented in accordance with the accreditation requirements and policies of the Accreditation Council for Continuing Medical Education (ACCME) through the joint providership of AKH Inc., Advancing Knowledge in Healthcare and the Jewish Federation of Greater Pittsburgh. AKH Inc., Advancing Knowledge in Healthcare is accredited by the ACCME to provide continuing medical education for physicians.

AKH Inc., Advancing Knowledge in Healthcare designates this live activity for a maximum of 2.0 AMA PRA Category 1 Credit(s)™. Physicians should claim only the credit commensurate with the extent of their participation in the activity.

Physician Assistants:
NCCPA accepts AMA PRA Category 1 Credit™ from organizations accredited by ACCME.

TIME: All classes 6:15 - 8:30 p.m. Dinner will be provided.

PLACE: Jewish Healthcare Foundation, QI2T Center
650 Smithfield St., 26th Floor
Pittsburgh, PA 15222

COST: $36 per session, or $90 for the series.
