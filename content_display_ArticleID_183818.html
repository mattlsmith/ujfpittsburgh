2006 Pacesetters Event: An Evening with Josh Malina



More than 300 people attended the 2006 Annual Community Campaign Pacesetters Thank You Event featuring remarks by actor Joshua Malina, star of the Emmy Award-winning NBC-TV show "The West Wing."

Those in attendance were being acknowledged for their commitments of $1,000 or more to the 2006 Campaign, at this special event that was underwritten by the Ira and Nanette Gordon Endowment Fund of the UJF Foundation.

In discussing "How to Make it in Hollywood and Remain a Mensch," Josh Malina related anecdotes from his early years in Jewish day school and throughout his acting career. He also expressed his love of Israel and his sadness that more celebrities do not use their visibility and prestige to Israel’s benefit.

Other highlights of the event included:

  • Presentation of the 2006 Campaigner of the Year Award to the participants of Mission Atidim, the recent Young Adult Mission to Israel that resulted in a 160% person-for-person increase in Campaign commitments.
  • A Campaign update during which 2006 Campaign Chair Nancy Ostrow reported that the Campaign had crossed the $10.4 million mark, on its way to a goal of $12.25 million, with an extra boost from the Citizens Bank matching grant.
  • Screening of a moving film from Ethiopia showcasing the needs and important work being done for Ethiopian Jews, and personal remarks about the Operation Promise Campaign from Meryl Ainsman, who visited Ethiopia.
  • A tribute by UJF Chair Barbara Burstin to Ira and Nanette Gordon, whose generosity and foresight led to the creation of an endowment fund in the UJF Foundation that has underwritten this event and will provide funding in perpetuity for the Pacesetters event.
  • A silent auction for two signed prints by nationally-acclaimed artist Burton Morris, in conjunction with the UJF Online Auction – and on-site bidding for the Auction, with computer terminals set up and several of the items up for bid on display.
