"The Partnership Report"- An On The Scene Bulletin From Karmiel & Misgav

July 25, 2006


To our dear friends and partners,

Today is Tuesday and the 14th day of the war.
Throughout the morning and afternoon hours, air raid sirens were heard throughout the north, six of them in Karmiel and Misgav communities. Until now, there has been no report of Katyusha rockets falling within the city of Karmiel and/or the Misgav communities. Fortunately, all of the rounds were spotted falling in open fields.

Danny Sanderson & Friends performing in a Misgav Shelter
Over the past two weeks, we have learned to live with a new reality, one that requires us to stay in our homes, in "secure rooms" and bomb shelters, often for many hours at a time, to "teach our ears" to hear the various sirens, incoming and outgoing explosive rounds of rockets, missiles and artillery shells. How to deal with fear and do our shopping and other errands hastily, outside the safety of the shelters, before the next round.

The municipalities have also entered into an "emergency routine" of daily update and emergency meetings, touring the city and the region, meetings with area residents, hosting visiting VIP's, not to mention all the "regular stuff" that still needs to be dealt with.

Shaul Mofaz with Adi Eldar and City Officials
Today we were visited by Shaul Mofaz and Meir Sheetrit, the Ministers of Transportation and Construction & Housing who promised to make a renewed effort towards extending the current railroad line from Akko to Karmiel. We were also visited by Rabbi Yechiel Eckstein and the management staff of the International Fellowship of Christians and Jews, who took it upon themselves to fund and supply hot meals to the elderly and other needy individuals throughout the north.

Yesterday in a daily update to council employees Erez Kriesler, mayor of the Misgav Regional Council said, "we are making every effort to try and create a certain normalcy within the continuing emergency situation. Our primary efforts are currently directed towards assisting the communities in issues relating to their defense and emergency preparedness along with a wide range of activities in the field of organized activities for our children, teenagers and adults forced to sit in shelters and secure rooms."

Rabbi Eckstein being greeted by Karmiel city inspectors
In an effort to restore a basic sense of normalcy to the area, the region's swimming team has renewed practice, the tennis team participated in a training camp at the Herzliya Community Center and a group of Misgav young people went for a day of fun in the center of the country.

In spite of the difficult security situation in the north, the community soccer project sponsored by the British Arsenal Football Club continues its activities. Today the Galilee team left for a 3-day training camp held at the "Hadassah Neurim" Youth Village in advance of their participation in the upcoming "Arsenal International Football Festival" scheduled to take place in London during the beginning of August.

Misgav Community Center Sports Department
This project is a joint initiative co-sponsored by the British Football Club and the Misgav Community Center Sports Department. Its objective is to promote cooperation between the various population sectors in the region, by means of an annual soccer program in which seven mixed teams were established with each team composed of 14 Arab and Jewish players. The teams all participated in a tournament in which teams from Akko and Nahaf-Karmiel played in the finals. At the closing ceremonies held in May, 16 players (2 from each participating municipalities) were selected to represent the Galilee at the "Arsenal International Football Festival" scheduled to take place in August. Some 150 teenage players from the Misgav Regional Council, Karmiel, Akko, Lower Galilee Regional Council, Nahaf, Aibleen, and Deboriah participated in the program.

Today as well, I want to present you with a personal view of how area residents are coping with the current situation. The following is an article, published yesterday in Maariv, written about the residents of Hararit, a unique Misgav community.

A New Initiative In The Galilee: To Beat Nasrallah By Means Of Transcendental Meditation

The Brave Flyers

Fed Up With The War? Take A Deep Breath  In The Meditating Village Of Hararit In The Galilee They Believe That Only Yogistic Flight Will Succeed In Deflecting The Katyusha Rockets From Their Lethal Flight Path. Kassam Rockets As Well "Olmert Needs To Call Us Up, Immediately"

By Nadav Eyal

As the days pass, there is an ever-decreasing interest among the public and press in the rounds of Katyusha rockets striking the north. Our sensitivity towards the sirens, falling rounds, burnt cars and pocketed roofs grows dull. The current state of national emotion demands to hear stories telling us IDF heroism as we hear daily on the TV that the home front is steadfast and strong. Maybe now is the time, when conventional warfare and means of communication are at their fullest intensity to turn to the alternative from the Galilee.

The road up to Hararit is a long drive, but the scenery is no doubt worth it. Hararit was first established by a group dedicated to the principles of Transcendental Meditation (TM). The community is in the Galilee, well within rocket range however, it has yet to be targeted.

According to the residents of Hararit, the story is clear; they believe that meditation has the power to prevent any phenomenon the stems from stress and hostility, even warfare. Since there definitely is a war going on, we went to Hararit, but the surprise was on us. "Everyone left for the Kinneret", we were told by Orit Barak, "my husband too.  For the past two weeks, everyone's been meditating non-stop in an effort to improve the situation, since the fighting. You'll find them at Ginnosar."

"I'll Spell It For You Later"
Alex Kutai, the head of the movement in Israel, said that ten days before the beginning of fighting began a warning was received that the world was headed for a difficult time. In fact, he said, this is what has happened. "On the day before it all began, July 11, we all gathered at the Nof Ginnosar Hotel and from that moment on began to practice meditation and yogistic flight", Kutai said. (Feel it, he says, this is Transcendental Meditation, I'll spell it for you later. This is a type of meditation that its impact has been scientifically researched).

This is not a religion, or mysticism, he emphasized strongly. There are more than 600 scientific studies! Of these studies, 52 of them have proven that meditation is capable of solving conflicts, including one that was conducted here in Israel within the framework of the war in Lebanon. Tell us, we asked, why here in Ginnosar? Katyusha rockets are falling here in the area. This isn't a place for meditation. "We can't be harmed", responded Kutai, there is a defensive umbrella protecting us, and the missiles fly straight to Tiberias." He sounded satisfied. "Then it's a shame you're not in Tiberias", someone said, the umbrella would open up over them".

Kutai, a serious and honest man, did not surrender to cynicism. It is impossible to be in Tiberias, he explained, the hotels there were constructed in the wrong way. Nof Ginnosar was empty and the Kinneret blue and stormy. Dr. Reuven Zelinkovski, a reserve colonel in Army Intelligence, an electronics engineer is standing on the lawn and explaining that it is possible to end the war immediately. Tomorrow morning. The rockets, he said, won't fly. We'll be protected from the Kassam rockets as well. If only the government would try, he said, we would be able to solve the conflict forever.

Carefully we asked how the conflict could be solved. "If you bring together a certain number of people who practice meditation and yogistic flight that is the square root of 1% of the world's population, then the war will end tomorrow morning ", said Dr. Zelinkovski. Between 256 and 300 people who practice meditation and it will all be over. If that's so, we said why don't you bring them all together and finish it. That's a problem, Kutai and Zelinkovski admitted frustratingly. These people all have previous commitments, jobs, not all of them can afford a hotel. All together, 350 people practice in Israel today, but it's not a simple thing to recruit all of them. "One even told me", said one of the organizers with great sorrow, "that she is in a weight loss course."

You need to understand, said Dr. Daniel Glicker that this would cost less than the tail wing of an F-16 to fund a group of people to meditate for the whole country and guarantee peace. We call upon the Prime Minister to draft us immediately, said those present. No military solution has succeeded.

As we said our goodbyes to the nice people of Ginnosar, we imagined Olmert calling a special press conference to call for a special meditation call-up. It's an interesting thought. It accompanied us all the way back to rocket attacked Nahariya.

That's all for today, let us pray that quiet will be restored and peace and security will return to the region and the country.

Sincerely yours,
Yael Raich
