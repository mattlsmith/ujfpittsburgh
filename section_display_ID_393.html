Why a Woman's Gift

Women's giving is fiscal evidence of the influence women exert as full partners and decision-makers in the family, the community, and the nation.

Women can impact Jewish political power: U.S. government leaders count the number of givers when they measure support for local communities and Israel.

Women have an obligation to practice the mitzvah of giving tzedakah in their own right.

Money is the influence; the more women give in their own name, the greater their credibility and power as leaders and policy makers.

National Women’s Philanthropy

Inspired by Tradition, Tzedakah, and Tikkun Olam, National Women’s Philanthropy of the United Jewish Communities is dedicated to the survival, continuity and connections of our North American communities, Israel and the Jewish people. National Women's Philanthropy supports the vision of the United Jewish Communities embracing Jewish Renaissance, Human Service and Social Policy, Israel/Overseas, and Financial Resource Development.  more
