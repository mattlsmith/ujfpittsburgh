UJF Names Four to Key Positions



Four members of the United Jewish Federation staff have been named to key positions.

Daniel O. Brandeis, previously UJF Foundation Director, has been named Director of the UJF Foundation/ Financial Resource Development. In his new role, Brandeis will continue to direct the UJF Foundation and will oversee all financial resource development efforts by the Federation.

Brian S. Eglash, who has served as Director of Agency Endowment Development for the UJF Foundation, is the new UJF Campaign Director. Eglash will direct all efforts associated with the UJF Annual Community Campaign.

Edie Naveh, who has served as Director of Community and Public Affair for UJF, will succeed Linda Hurwitz as Director of the Holocaust Center of Pittsburgh. In this capacity, Edie will oversee all educational, programmatic and development efforts of the Holocaust Center.

Samantha Kriendler, who has served as a Senior Development Associate, has been named Assistant Campaign Director. Kriendler will work with Eglash directing the operations of the UJF Annual Community Campaign.

"These additions bring great diversity and strength to our staff and will help us achieve great accomplishments in the coming years," said Jeffrey H. Finkelstein, UJF President.

Brandeis, an attorney who specializes in estate planning and taxation, is a native of Pittsburgh and a graduate of the University of Pittsburgh School of Law. He also holds a specialized advanced legal degree in income taxation from Villanova University School of Law. He earned a Bachelor's degree from Colby College in Waterville, Me.

Prior to joining the UJF staff, Brandeis served as House Counsel to Legacy Advisors, LLC, a Philadelphia firm specializing in estate and business succession planning. He also worked as an attorney specializing in estate planning for Klett, Rooney, Leiber & Schorling in Pittsburgh. Additionally, Brandeis held financial management positions with Metropolitan Life Insurance Company and Bear Stearns.

Eglash joined the UJF staff in 1997 and launched the Federation's now successful Agency Endowment Development program. Previously he served as the UJF Director of Allocations and Overseas Operations and Assistant Director of Planning.

A native of Milwaukee, WI, Eglash immigrated to Israel in 1990. While there he held several positions with the Jewish Agency For Israel, including Coordinator of Project OTZMA, Director of Sheirut La'am and Director of Long Term English Language programs.

A graduate of the University of Wisconsin, Eglash holds a Master's Degree in Educational Policy and Community Studies specializing in Middle Eastern/Contemporary Jewish Studies and a Bachelors degree in History and Political Science.

Naveh, one of the most senior Jewish communal professionals at UJF, moves to the Holocaust Center as part of UJF's effort to continue the Center's excellent program, established by Hurwitz.

Naveh, who served as the Holocaust Center's Director in the mid 1980s, brings to the position academic credentials relating to Holocaust studies and significant development experience.

Since 2001, Naveh served as Director of Community and Public Affairs, a successor to the UJF's Community Relations Department. She began her UJF career in 1984 as a Community Relations and Campaign Associate. Prior to her employment at UJF she served as Assistant Director of B'nai B'rith Hillel at the University of Pittsburgh.

Naveh holds a PhD from the University of Pittsburgh in Theater Arts, specializing in Holocaust drama. She also holds a Masters in Theater Arts from Louisana State University specializing in the War Propaganda Plays of Lillian Hellman. Naveh holds another Masters In Public Administration from the University of Pittsburgh and a Bachelors from Louisiana State University.

She is a past recipient of the Doris and Leonard H. Rudolph Jewish Communal Professional Award, the highest award presented annually to a Jewish communal professional in Pittsburgh.

Naveh also has received numerous other awards relating to her community relations and Holocaust work. Among the most notable was recognition by Pittsburgh's Polish community for bringing to Pittsburgh the International Auschwitz Exhibit.

Naveh is Vice Chair of the National Catholic Center on Holocaust Education at Seton Hill University. She was the first chair of the American Friends of Action Reconciliation Service for Peace, a program which provides young Germans an opportunity to do community service throughout the world to rebuild the countries which had been brutalized by the Nazi Holocaust.

Kriendler, a native of New York, has held UJF positions in the Community Building and Development arenas. As Senior Development Associate, she held key staff responsibilities in the UJF Community Campaign. Previously she was a Planning and Development Associate, where among her responsibilities was staffing the initial phases of the Jewish Learning Initiative.

Kriendler, a lawyer, is a graduate of Brooklyn Law School and holds a Bachelor's degree from the University of Wisconsin.
