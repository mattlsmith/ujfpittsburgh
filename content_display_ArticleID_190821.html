North American Israel Emergency Campaign to be Launched Monday



In an effort to meet the critical needs faced by the Israeli people in the wake of relentless Hezbollah rocket attacks, the United Jewish Federation of Pittsburgh is expected to join Monday the launch of a coordinated North American campaign to raise more than $300 million through the UJF's national organization, United Jewish Communities (UJC).

The Israel Emergency Campaign, approved by the UJC Wednesday, will provide immediate and direct relief to the Israeli people who have been terrorized over the past three weeks by rocket attacks causing death, injury and destruction throughout major communities in Northern Israel.

"This campaign will bring together in a coordinated approach, the many independent fundraising efforts that are underway by local federations throughout the United States," said Barbara Burstin, UJF Chair of the Board. "Pending approval by the UJF Board of Trustees at a special meeting Monday, the Israel Emergency Campaign will run in tandem with the 2007 UJF Annual Community Campaign.

The UJF Trustees will also be addressed at that time by Prime Minister Ehud Olmert, via a closed-circuit broadcast, to outline the challenges Israel faces. Following the prime minister's remarks, the Trustees will hear from Karnit Goldwasser, the wife of Ehud "Udi" Goldwasser, the Israel Defense Forces reservist who was one of the two IDF soldiers Hezbollah captured last month, igniting the current conflict.

The broadcast will be made available for viewing through the UJF's web site, www.uffpittsburgh.org, early next week.

Thus far, UJC reports that $23 million has been raised for Israel through the federation system’s many individual campaigns. Pittsburgh’s total for Israel relief has crossed the $500,000 mark. 

"Because of the immediate needs in Israel and to maximize our volunteer efforts and community resources, we will soon launch and accelerate both the 2007 Annual Community Campaign with the Israel Emergency Campaign," said Nancy Ostrow, chair of the UJF Annual Community Campaign. "While our campaign leadership has not yet determined a goal for the 2007 Annual Campaign and the Israel Emergency Campaign, we all need to consider stretching ourselves for Israel while increasing our annual campaign commitment to meet our ever increasing needs locally and throughout the world." she added.

"We are in a crisis situation in Israel," said Edgar Snyder, chair of the UJF’s Israel and World Jewry Commission. "More than 1 million people in northern Israel have been living each day in bomb shelters. These are very old facilities that do not have air conditioning and have very poor ventilation. It’s frightening," he added.

"Just as we have done in all other periods of crisis that Israel has faced, we again must do all we can," Snyder said. "Our own Partnership 2000 communities of Karmiel and Misgav have been profoundly affected," he added "Scores of buildings have been hit by rockets resulting in millions of dollars in damage, leaving people homeless and devastating the economy. The residents have been spending most days and nights in bomb shelters."

The UJF recently sent $30,000 to Karmiel and Misgav for the purchase of bulletproof vests and helmets for volunteers who are scurrying around trying to help the elderly and take care of the other needs in the region.

Some of the immediate needs for the funds in Israel include:

  • The movement of 17,000 Jewish, Druz, Arab-Israeli and new immigrant children from the northern areas of Israel to overnight camps in the center of the country in order to ensure their safety and provide them with relief from the constant bombings.
  • Providing for the psychological and physical well-being of immigrants and elderly forced to spend their days in hot, crowded shelters and "safe rooms".
  • Supplying shelter emergency kits equipped with necessities such as food, portable chemical toilets, emergency equipment and other items such as televisions with VCRs or DVDs along with books and games for children. 
  • Equipping bomb shelters with portable air conditioning units and emergency lighting for those faced with staying in the bomb shelters for extended periods of time.

The funding is already allowing the Jewish Agency for Israel to bring more than 6,000 youth to summer camps and youth villages in safer areas further south, including hundreds of recent immigrants from Ethiopia; help the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee through its AMEN youth volunteer program provide entertainment and activities for 10,000 children in bomb shelters; deliver support services such as meals-on-wheels and medicine to 10,000 elderly and disabled people in assisted living centers and at home; ensure that residential shelters have essential supplies; and boost Israel Community Center Association outreach efforts to 15,000 children.
