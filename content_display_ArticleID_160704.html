2005 Annual Campaign Crosses $12 million
The United Jewish Federation's 2005 Annual Campaign has reached its goal of $12 million according to an announcement made to the UJF Board of Trustees by Campaign Chair Arnie Gefsky.

"Achieving this goal, with the expectation that we can exceed it, enables us to continue providing necessary human services and educational needs here in Pittsburgh and across the world. The entire community can share in the satisfaction of knowing that we came together to accomplish the largest Campaign goal in UJF history," Gefsky said.

Campaign Director Brian Eglash said, "Our goal this year was ambitious but we knew that there were individuals in Pittsburgh, Israel and overseas depending on us, people who rely on us for food, medicine, clothing, education and other critical services. These serious needs are what drive our leaders, our staff and the entire community to meet the goal, no matter how demanding."

Also announced was the UJF Foundation achievement of $16 million in contributions to new and existing funds exceeding its goal of $13.5 million.

CMU President Addresses UJF Delegates
In recent years, Carnegie Mellon University has worked closely with the United Jewish Federation at many different levels, particularly related to Partnership 2000. Recently, relations between the university and the Jewish community have been strained due to a series of ant-semitic and/or anti-Zionist speakers at the university. As a result, the UJF invited Dr. Jared Cohon, president of Carnegie Mellon University, to address the UJF Board of Delegates and discuss the many concerns in the Jewish community related to the recent speakers. The Board of Delegates represent the leadership of every synagogue, Jewish organization and agency in the community.

An informative and productive exchange of views took place between Dr. Cohon and members of the Board of Delegates. Dr. Cohon shared his desire to work closely with our community to ensure that Jewish students are comfortable on the campus. As a result, several good suggestions were made and well received by Dr. Cohon on how the University and our community can work together in a positive and productive way at many different levels.

Birthright Israel, First-hand Account

My name is Rachel Goykhman and I am a student at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU). Within the course of one semester, three different speakers, each in his own way anti-Israel and anti-Semitic, were sponsored by various student organizations, as well as by the university itself. Teen Leadership 2005 In Full Swing
Twelve Pittsburghers and 13 Israelis from Karmiel and Misgav are currently participating in a 5-week Teen Leadership program. The goal of the program is to provide Jewish, American and Israeli teens, who have demonstrated leadership qualities, with a fun, yet intensive leadership experience in Israeli/Diaspora relations through living together and learning about each other's lives.
