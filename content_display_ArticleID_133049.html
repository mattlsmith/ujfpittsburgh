UJF News

Board of Delegates Meeting
Thursday, November 18, 2004, at 7:00 p.m.
United Jewish Federation.

As members of the UJF Board of Delegates this meeting fulfills two of the delegates responsibilities: it offers you the opportunity to continue to guide the community visioning process -- "20/20 Vision: The Jewish Future for Jewish Pittsburgh." You will also have the opportunity to learn first hand about how our efforts are making an impact in Israel and around the world through a unique new program the "New Faces of Aliyah."

Join us before the meeting for a special wine and cheese reception hosted by PNC Bank in honor of Dan Shapira, recipient of the newly created PNC Community Builders Award.

The agenda will include the following:

• The newly created PNC Community Builders Award presentation to Dan Shapira.
• A report by UJC consultant Ann Cohen, regarding next steps in implementing our "20/20 Vision: The Jewish Future for Jewish Pittsburgh." You, as Delegates, launched this process, and we are now ready to move forward into the second phase and we are seeking your input.
• Presentations from two young adult immigrants to Israel, Yael Agala originally from Ethiopia, and Marina Galper, born in Russia. Both are part of the "New Faces of Aliyah," a program sponsored by the Jewish Agency for Israel. They are coming to Pittsburgh to share their moving stories of aliyah and will help us understand how our efforts make a difference in Israel and throughout the world.
• A message from Jeff Finkelstein, UJF's newly appointed President and CEO.

Please contact Sally Stein by phone, 412-992-5243, or by email, sstein@ujf.net to R.S.V.P.

UJF - High Ranking From Charity Navigator Charity Navigator, America's premier independent evaluator of charities, has ranked the United Jewish Federation 5th among the 27 charities it reviewed in Pittsburgh. Overall, Pittsburgh nonprofits rank first in the nation for efficiency, knocking Cleveland from that post this year.

Charity Navigator bases its ratings on its independent evaluation of financial information provided through the charity's Internal Revenue Service tax returns. Financial performance is analyzed in the areas of organizational efficiency, fund-raising efficiency, fund-raising expenses, program expenses, administrative expenses, organizational capacity, primary revenue growth and working capital. The group also examined charities' growth to assess their ability to sustain their programs over time.

The UJF Audit Committee plays a key role in ensuring the quality of financial reporting and acts on behalf of the UJF's Trustees to ensure the integrity of the Federation financial statements.

Charity Navigator evaluates the financial health of more than 3,300 of America's largest charities.
