"The Partnership Report"- An On The Scene Bulletin From Karmiel & Misgav July 18, 2006



To our dear friends and partners,

Last night thankfully passed with no significant damage or injuries. The single barrage of Katyusha rockets that hit our region all fell in open fields in the area between Karmiel and Misgav.

Large numbers of area residents have left their homes, temporarily, moving to safer quarters in the central or southern sectors of the country until things calm down.

Immigrant children from Jewish Agency absorption centers throughout the north, including those from the center in Karmiel were temporarily evacuated to agency youth villages out of rocket range where they will have the opportunity to participate in a wide range of enjoyable summer camp activities in peace and quiet.

"Partnership 2000" Mega Youth Mission says "Bye for Now"
Young People from Karmiel, Misgav & Pittsburgh sharing a P2K Moment in a Jerusalem Forest
After three fun filled and enjoyable days together with young people from the Karmiel-Misgav region the American delegation returned home to Pittsburgh, and the Israelis to the Galilee.

Although they had only three days together, it was clear to all of us that the young people had developed into a unified group that is seriously looking forward to the reciprocal visit to Pittsburgh and their renewed meeting scheduled for the beginning of August.

   
  
Rockets Hit ORT Braude- Disrupt Summer Programs
The Results of Katyusha Rocket Attacks on ORT Braude
A Katyusha rocket barrage hit the ORT Braude College of Engineering in Karmiel on Saturday, destroying a number of campus offices, shattering windows and pock marking other buildings with shrapnel. Another rocket damaged the ORT Braude Physics Laboratories at the nearby Karmiel Science Park.

At the time of the attack, some 60 students resided in nearby dormitories but none was injured. Shimon Hacker, Administrative Head of Organization and Finance immediately evacuated them to safer quarters.

The rockets were among some of the 100 Katyusha missiles fired by Hezbollah at northern Israel on Saturday.

A spokesman for ORT Israel said, “We would like to thank ORT Israel’s many friends around the world who have called and written to express their identification and solidarity with us and our students.”

 

"Standing Steadfast"- Adi Eldar
Published in Maariv Sunday July 16, 2006

When we heard the Katyusha rockets slamming into the neighboring Arab village of Majd al Crum, I knew that they were aiming for my city of Karmiel.

The first barrage struck on Friday, four more throughout the Shabbat, and no one knows how many will rain upon us in the coming days and weeks.

Since my arrival in Karmiel in 1975, rockets had never been fired in our direction. In fact, throughout its history rockets had never been fired on Karmiel. However now, the situation has changed. The reality of rockets screaming over our heads has been forced upon us bring with them injury, loss of life and property damage.

My city has been struck by rockets and is under attack. While on one hand, it is impossible to believe that this is happening to us, on the other our thoughts are directed towards questions concerning the state of public shelters. Are they open and suitable for housing families? How can we help residents whose homes were damaged? What can we do to prevent further damage? As mayor, what can I do to limit the damage?

In normal times, we'd be celebrating the Karmiel Dance Festival, but now no one knows if or when this will take place.

We all share great concern for our sons and daughters on all fronts.  We are especially concerned for the fate of Sergeant Major Dov Stranschuss, a resident of our city, who is MIA together with a number of his friends after a missile attack on his Israeli naval vessel off the Lebanese coast  (in the meantime Dov's body was recovered and brought to a proper burial in Karmiel on Tuesday July 18, 2006).

We must all remember that the final objective of the terrorist Nasrallah is to strike at the State of Israel and annihilate its citizens. Our anger is great, our frustration and concern ever growing; nonetheless, our determination only grows stronger.

We must not capitulate or show weakness in the face of an enemy such as the Hezbollah. The words "escape" or "retreat" cannot be in our lexicon. This is truly a war in defense of our homes and we are telling Prime Minister Ehud Olmert, Minister of Defense Amir Peretz, members of the government, the IDF and other security services that the home front is behind you! Don't dare stop what you've begun. The nation stands steadfast in the face of battle! We, the mayors of Israel's cities, support the steps that you've taken and back your efforts. Don't weaken. With great might and wisdom, destroy the Hezbollah!
   
• The writer is mayor of Karmiel and chairman of the Local Authorities Union of Israel.
  

From Karmiel's Schools
Shrapnel Damage to One of Karmiel's Schools
On the ORT Cramim Karmiel school website, the school principal, Yehoshua Zohar, wrote, “Unlike previous conflicts, this time Karmiel is also in the line of fire. Right now, we are once again fighting for our existence. It was not something we wanted, but we have a new war in Lebanon, after the abduction of two of our soldiers…. Our success in this current battle depends largely on how strong we are at home. In other words, how each and every one of us behaves. Our enemies believe that the shelling of our cities and towns will break our spirit. They believe we are weak… but they couldn’t be more wrong.” While writing these words, quite a number of rockets have already fallen on Karmiel. We must be strong so that the IDF can complete their missions – to get our soldiers back and create a strategic change along our northern border. Have courage. We have been though worse times and yet prevailed.”

At ORT Megadim in Karmiel, the school principal, David Atmor, addressed his students, “Today we are all at the front line and it is not a pleasant experience. On Saturday, a rocket fell at the entrance to the gymnasium at school, also the house of one of our teachers was damaged, and worst of all, a few minutes ago we were informed of the loss of Sergeant Major Dov Stranschuss, the father of Sapir from our Junior High, who was among those killed on the Navy vessel. These are hard times for us all. There is no shame in being worried, but we have to remain strong.” The forum we have opened invites you to share your sentiments and questions and our counselors are here to assist you.

 
A Message from Scott Mann Lay-Chairman Partnership 2000 Misgav
Dear Partners,
The Results of Katyusha Rocket Attacks on Tal El
Because of your concern and overwhelming identification with the Misgav-Karmiel Region, I have been asked to write a personal account of what is happening around me and send it on to you in Pittsburgh.

As you already know, for the first time our region is dealing with missiles falling in our own "back yard." Nevertheless, this has not caused the panic and chaos our enemies were hoping for.

Those of you who have visited my home have seen the spectacular view of Karmiel from my living room window. Well, this past Shabbat afternoon my wife and I got to see four katyusha rockets falling on Karmiel in "real time".

In my village, Eshcahr, a family was celebrating the Bar Mitzvah of their son, and had brought a whole busload of religious guests from the center of the country. It turned out to be a Bar Mitzvah they'll never forget, accompanied by really impressive "sound effects" and an unusual "fireworks" display!

Thank G-d no one in the Misgav-Karmiel region has been seriously injured or killed.

Yesterday I traveled to Ben Gurion airport to pick up some of my family who were arriving for a two-week visit, which was to include a week in the region. The meeting at the airport was a bit surreal—the huge arrival hall, which most of you know, was just about empty! Understandably, there were very few people waiting for out of town guests to arrive. As for my guests, unfortunately, the circumstances have required a change in itinerary, and they'll be spending all their time in Israel no further north than Tel Aviv.

From the airport I took my guests to Jerusalem, which seemed to be quite normal. Last evening we drove back home with hardly any traffic moving north.

When I was a lawyer in Chicago I can't recall the courts being closed on account of missile attacks, but on Sunday I had a hearing in Haifa postponed for that very reason. Most banks and offices in Haifa and its suburbs are closed, and most residents are in shelters.

My office is in Afula, a city that has also been the target of several missiles (resulting in property damage only). However, in Afula, most offices are open and my staff saw no reason not to come in to work.

Of course, all of us involved in Partnership here have been extremely concerned with the 93 kids from Pittsburgh who were in Israel. I am sure you are all aware of the decisions that were made and why, but I would just like to commend the staff for dealing with a constantly changing, difficult situation. As a parent of one of the Israeli kids who is participating in the program, I can vouch for the disappointment our kids are feeling—especially those not scheduled to come to Pittsburgh for the continuation of the program. The kids had a wonderful 3 days together, but this is not what we hoped and planned for when we all excitedly planned and budgeted this year's Mega-mission. I only hope that some of the programming we'd planned to do here can now be done during the group's time in Pittsburgh.

The main thing I want to convey to you is the strong spirit of our people and in our region. Just about everybody is of the same opinion—we are willing to put up with this situation for as long as it takes, in the hope that the end result will be real harm to Hezbollah and elimination of their hostile presence on our northern border.

The many e-mails and phone calls we've received from our Pittsburgh "family" have been very welcome. We know you are with us and praying for a quick and positive resolution of this situation. I pray for the return to normalcy that will allow our region to continue to prosper, and know that Partnership 2000 will be there to help us.

With great affection,
Scott Mann
Lay-Chairman Partnership 2000-Misgav

That's all for today, let us pray that quiet will be restored and peace and security will return to the region and the country.

Sincerely yours,
Yael Raich- Jewish Agency for Israel
