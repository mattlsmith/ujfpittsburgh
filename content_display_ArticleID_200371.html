ISRAEL MAINTAINS FALAS MURA ALIYAH RATE, AFTER UJC ADVOCACY EFFORT
 
 
 

Nov. 10, 2006

 

 

This week our UJC Israel office received a letter from Raanan Dinur, director-general of Prime Minister Ehud Olmert's office, informing us that the Government of Israel has reversed its previous decision to reduce the rate of immigration of the Falas Mura in 2007 from 300 to 150 per month. Instead,  the government has committed to maintain the rate at 300 per month.

 

It is clear that pressure brought to bear by our federation leadership -- by you -- has played a critical role in this decision.
 
While the continuation of the current rate of immigration is still less than what the government of Prime Minister Ariel Sharon pledged in January 2005, it is an important achievement in pursuing the completion of the historic redemption of Ethiopian Jewry.
 
We remain engaged in supporting and assisting the preparation of the Falas Mura community in Ethiopia for their new lives in Israel with the help of the generous contributions that have been made to Operation Promise. We will remain steadfast in our commitment to bringing about the conclusion of this aliyah as quickly as possible.
 
Our thanks to you and your federations for your advocacy efforts in recent weeks and for your continued dedication to Ethiopian Jewry.  We look forward to seeing you at the General Assembly in Los Angeles.

 

 

Robert Goldberg

Chairman, UJC Board of Trustees

 

 

Howard Rieger

President and CEO, UJC
