A special learning session presented by RABBI DR. DANNY SCHIFF
Foundation Scholar

Sunday, March 27, 2016

10:00 AM

JCC Squirrel Hill
Levinson Hall
5738 Forbes Avenue | Pittsburgh, PA 15217

In this special learning session, as follow up to the Feb. 25 Community Conversation about the Refugee Crisis, Rabbi Schiff will explore the current political debate over immigration from the perspective of Jewish sources.

There are some who would seek to exclude all immigrants from those groups that might contain individuals who pose a threat to our nation. Given Judaism’s emphasis on saving life, is this a reasonable approach? If immigration threatens to change the ethos of a nation, as some analysts have posited, is that a reason to exclude? Utilizing a full range of Jewish sources, Rabbi Schiff will address the concerns of both sides of these difficult questions through the prism of our tradition.

The session — Sunday, March 27, 10 a.m., JCC Squirrel Hill — is free and open to the public but advance registration is encouraged.

Refreshments served. Dietary laws observed.

For more information, contact Eric Probola, 412-992-5247 or eprobola@jfedpgh.org.

Presented by the Jewish Community Foundation and the Community Relations Council of the Jewish Federation of Greater Pittsburgh.
