Our Good Work Lives On

in our tradition of caring for those in need

 

Children and teens are deprived of Jewish experiences

that could touch their souls and alter their sense of Jewishness for life… And, more and more, Jewish adults feel like “something is missing” – and yearn for a stronger connection to the community and to Jewish life.

 

  • $36 ($3/month) provides Jewish holiday meals at the Hillel Jewish University Center for a college student away from home and family
  • $250 provides updated computer software for day school kindergarten students learning their ABCs and their Aleph Bet
  • $480 ($40/month) introduces a child to Israeli culture during a week of JCC summer day camp
  • $2,400 ($200/month) underwrites classes for teens yearning to continue their Jewish learning at the School for Advanced Jewish Studies
  • $5,000 subsidizes an entire year of supplemental Hebrew and Sunday School at a local synagogue through Federation Education Enrichment Fund (FEEF)

 

  Live Genersously.

  Pledge today.

To keep our community strong and vibrant we must assure that Jewish learning is accessible and affordable to Jews of all backgrounds and affiliations.  We must provide opportunties for young adults to connect to the community and develop leadership skills that will enable them to take our community forward in the coming decades.  UJF Annual Campaign dollars support a vast range of Jewish learning experiences that keep our culture and tradition dynamic and meaningful to the young and old, individuals and families.
