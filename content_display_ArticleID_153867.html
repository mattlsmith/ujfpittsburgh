Pacesetters Celebrate in Good Taste



Donors to the 2005 United Jewish Federation Community Campaign "celebrated in good taste" at the annual Pacesetters Thank You Event, along with special guest Jerry Greenfield, co-founder of Ben & Jerry’s Homemade, Inc.

Jerry Greenfield and his long-time friend and business partner Ben Cohen are the men behind one of the most talked-about and least conventional success stories in American business: the two built a storefront venture into a $160 million publicly-held ice cream empire. Authors of the best seller Ben & Jerry’s Double Dip: Lead with Your Values and Make Money, Too, Greenfield and Cohen have received awards for their company’s commitment to social responsibility.

Jerry shared humorous anecdotes about how the business came about – following the two friends’ completion of a $5 Penn State correspondence course in ice cream making – and elaborated on the fulfillment of running a business that, in addition to amassing assets, also helped improve the community and the lives of others.

The Pacesetter's program is underwritten by the Ira and Nanette Gordon Endowment Fund of the UJF Foundation. The event was for donors of $1,000 or more to the 2005 UJF Annual Community Campaign.
